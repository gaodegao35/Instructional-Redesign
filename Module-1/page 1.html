Scenario
You run a bicycle repair shop. It has been six months. Tools are scattered, parts are stacked in corners, and you have been keeping most things in your head. It isn't really working anymore. You want to get more systematic about how you run this thing.

Three different moments, the same realization: To get things organized, it's time to write things down and create a list.
Scenario
You decide to look into insuring the things you use in the shop. You call Maria - the agent your hardware-store friend uses.

"Great. Eventually I'll need a detailed list - every wrench, every tube, every bracket, with a value for each. But to draft a policy I just need a summary. Group your stuff in a way that makes sense, give me a rough value for each group, and we'll work from that."

You start sorting things into different groups.
